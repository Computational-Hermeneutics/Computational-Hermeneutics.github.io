Computational Hermeneutics

Research instruments for computational close reading

A family of research instruments that bring the apparatus of textual criticism and interpretation to computational artefacts — to code as much as to text.

Where the established traditions of philology gave us the witness, the siglum, the copy-text, the apparatus criticus, and the transposition, Computational Hermeneutics asks what those instruments become when the object of reading is software, a corpus, or a generated text. The tools here are local-first, interpretively serious, and built for close reading rather than distant measurement.

Instruments

Source Variorum v0.3.0

A braided collation workbench. Two or more witnesses of a text are set side by side with a central braid of ribbons showing matches, additions, deletions, substitutions, and transpositions — for source code and for prose alike — with an auto-generated critical apparatus.
